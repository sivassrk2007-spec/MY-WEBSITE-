Why Outdoor Identity Demands Its Own Playbook

The Construction Site Is Not a Gap.
It Is the Brand's Largest Medium.

While the project is being built, the site is the only tangible evidence that the brand's promise is real. Every hoarding, wrap, and gate sign is simultaneously a sales tool, a regulatory obligation, and a neighbourhood reputation signal. Most developers treat site signage as a production problem. The ones who win treat it as a brand strategy.

What Is at Stake — The Gap in Current Site Branding Practice

The Branding 360° touchpoint matrix lists "site hoardings" as one row among thirty-plus items. This document expands that single row into a complete outdoor identity system. The gap between current practice and what this system defines is not cosmetic — it is the difference between a construction site that erodes brand equity over two years and one that builds it month by month. A crane at 40 metres is a 360-degree brand flag visible from 1.5 kilometres. A stale hoarding in October still showing the January launch render tells every passerby that this developer cannot maintain what they have promised.

"The construction site that looks built before it is built — that is the outdoor identity system working at its highest level. Every hoarding, every wrap, every gate board is a vote in the market's verdict on whether this developer can be trusted to deliver."

01
Visual Dominance Architecture

The Hoarding Hierarchy — Four Tiers, Four Cognitive Modes

A site hoarding system works as a concentric set of communication zones. As a prospect approaches — by car at 80 km/h, by auto at 30 km/h, on foot, then standing at the gate — entirely different cognitive modes are active. Each tier maps to a mode, a message load, and a format.

The Tier System — Message Load Rules

The tier purity rule is non-negotiable: each tier must contain only the message load defined for it. A Tier I approach board carrying floor counts, pricing, and RERA numbers is a Tier III gate board deployed at highway scale. It fails at every viewing distance — too cluttered to read at speed, and too shallow to satisfy at close range. Tier compression is the single most common outdoor branding error in Indian real estate.

Tier I / IV
Approach
Dominance
Brand + one claim. Read at 150–300 m. Designed for vehicular speed, not pedestrian inspection.
I

Formats at This Tier

Unipole boards (40×20 ft and above), highway gantries, and large billboard placements on arterial roads within 500 m of the site. These are the first brand impression for buyers arriving by vehicle. The brand mark and a single positioning claim is the complete message — everything else is clutter at this distance.

The Tier I Principle

At 150 m, a vehicle is 6–7 seconds away at 80 km/h. The brain processes brand colour and shape before it reads text. Lead with colour field dominance and the brand mark at maximum size. If your logo is not legible at this distance, it is not the board's failure — it is a logo design failure that outdoor simply exposes.

Message Load — Tier I Maximum

Brand mark + project name + one claim

Maximum three visual elements at Tier I. Brand mark, project name, and one positioning claim or visual. Adding a phone number, RERA number, or floor count at this tier means none of the three elements receive the visual weight they need to register at speed.

Approach Sequencing — Capture Before Inform

A prospect passing at highway speed has two exposure moments: the initial glance at 300 m (brand recognition) and the sustained view from 150–80 m (message absorption). The board must work at both distances simultaneously — brand colour dominance at 300 m, readable claim at 150 m. Design to the further distance first.

Micromarket Anchoring Effect

Tier I hoardings near the site do more than advertise the project — they anchor the local buyer's perception of the micromarket's price tier. A prominent unipole carrying "Residences from ₹2.8 Cr" resets the neighbourhood's price expectation for all subsequent projects in the vicinity. The hoarding is price positioning for the entire land parcel.

Tier II / IV
Site Perimeter
Narrative
Project identity + key specs. Read at 30–80 m. The linear storytelling surface that surrounds the entire site.
II

Formats at This Tier

Site perimeter flex panels on MS frame (standard 10×8 ft modules), scaffolding wraps, and crane banners. These formats are seen by pedestrians, slow-moving traffic, and neighbouring residents. They have significantly more dwell time than Tier I, and can carry project identity, USP statements, lifestyle visuals, and amenity references — but never compliance content.

The Perimeter Narrative Sequence

A prospect circling the site on foot or by auto reads the perimeter as a sequential story. Panel sequence must follow an arc: Awareness (brand identity, no text) → Promise (USP + lifestyle visual) → Proof (amenities + credentials) → Conversion (price anchor + QR + CTA). Never repeat the same panel. Never leave the narrative order to the printer.

Panel Sequence — The Four Acts

  • Panels 1–2 · Awareness — brand mark + project name, 80% visual, no CTA
  • Panels 3–5 · Promise — USP + lifestyle visual, max 12 words
  • Panels 6–8 · Proof — amenities + credentials, icon grid acceptable
  • Final panel · Conversion — price anchor + CTA + QR code UTM-tracked

Fascia Continuity Rule

The MS frame structure between panels must never be exposed. A continuous brand-colour fascia strip along the top of all perimeter panels creates a unified hoarding wall rather than a series of individual boards. The fascia strip also elevates perceived production quality and prevents the "patchwork fence" appearance that signals developer inexperience.

Seasonal Campaign Inserts

Perimeter panels 3–5 (the Promise zone) are the correct location for seasonal campaign inserts. A branded offer band added at the base of the panel — without reprinting the full board — activates festive season messaging at minimal production cost. The band is temporary; the underlying panel is permanent until the next content refresh cycle.

Legibility Engineering

Viewing-Distance Typography — The 1:35 Rule and What Follows It

Outdoor typography fails silently — the driver simply does not read it and the opportunity is lost. The rules below are derived from DIN 1450, OAAA guidelines, and field-calibrated for Indian conditions: glare, dust haze, monsoon light diffusion, and competing visual noise in dense urban corridors.

The 1:35 Baseline Rule — Minimum Headline Height by Distance

For every 1 metre of viewing distance, headline type must be a minimum 35 mm print height on substrate. Body copy follows the 1:20 ratio. These are floor minimums — for arterial roads with vehicles at 60+ km/h, apply the speed-adjusted factor. At 80 km/h a driver has approximately 2.25 seconds of comfortable reading time within 150 m. Each line of text requires approximately 0.4 seconds to decode. Maximum readable lines at speed: 5. Design to the approach road speed, not the standard.

Typography Rule
Weight
Mandates
Font weight is not a stylistic preference at outdoor scale — it is a legibility engineering decision.
W

Colour Contrast Floors

Outdoor contrast must compensate for glare, sky reflection, and substrate fade over 18 months. White or cream on deep navy or black: minimum 5.5:1 ratio. Mid-tone brand colour backgrounds: 6.5:1 minimum, white or near-black type only. Photography bleed backgrounds: 7.0:1 with text on solid panel overlay — never direct type on photo without panel. Backlit substrate: 4.5:1 is acceptable (light transmission adds effective contrast).

Script, Decorative, and Italic Rules

Script and decorative faces are prohibited at all tiers. Italic use is restricted to taglines only — never headlines at Tier I or II. Serif display faces are acceptable at Tier II headline level only, provided the weight is Bold or heavier. At Tier I, sans-serif Black (900 weight) is the only acceptable headline treatment. The stroke width of any Tier I headline character must be at least 12% of the cap height.

Tier I — Approach

Black / Ultra Bold only

Weight: 800–900. Tracking: +20 to +40/1000em. Sans-serif mandatory. Stroke width ≥12% cap height.
Tier II — Perimeter

Bold / Extra Bold

Weight: 600–800. Tracking: +10 to +25/1000em. Display serif in headline acceptable. Italic for tagline only.
Tier III — Gate

Medium to Bold

Weight: 400–700. Body tracking: +5 to +15/1000em. Serif body acceptable at 30pt+. No script or decorative.
Tier IV — Safety

Bold + ALL CAPS

Weight: 700 minimum. ALL CAPS always. ISO 3864 / IS 9457 colour coding mandatory. Bilingual: English + local language.

Material, Print & Structural Standards

Production Specifications — Flex, Backlit, and LED

Complete substrate selection logic, print resolution requirements, finishing standards, and structural mounting specifications for each production category. The most widely deployed format in Indian real estate — flex vinyl — is also the most frequently under-specified. Under-specification is the primary cause of premature substrate failure, colour shift, and post-monsoon replacements.

Substrate 01 / 03
Flex /
Vinyl Frontlit
The workhorse of site branding. Correctly specified, it performs for 24 months. Under-specified, it fails in the first monsoon.
◻

Substrate & Print Standards

340–440 gsm PVC flex, UV-stabilised, for all site applications. Below 340 gsm is packaging-grade material — it will scallop within 8 weeks on an outdoor frame. Print method: solvent or eco-solvent inkjet at 720 dpi at 1:1 scale. At final print size, minimum resolution is 60–80 dpi for boards viewed at 5 metres or beyond; 150 dpi minimum for gate boards viewed at 3 metres or closer. Cold laminate: 80 micron minimum, matte finish to eliminate solar glare.

Structure & Installation Standards

MS frame pipe: 2.5-inch diameter, 2.5 mm wall thickness minimum for all freestanding boards. RCC footing 3×3×3 ft for any freestanding structure above 8 ft total height. Eyelets every 50 cm on hem, reinforced at all four corners with additional fabric patch. Flex panels above 20×10 ft must include wind relief cuts or be produced on perforated mesh substrate — solid flex at this scale fails structurally in sustained wind above 40 km/h.

Seam & Join Specifications

Heat-weld seams only — cold adhesive joins delaminate within 3 months of outdoor exposure. Maximum panel width before seam: 2 metres. No seam may fall on the logo, a face, or a headline — seams must fall on background colour fields or neutral areas. Seam position must be reviewed at pre-production layout stage, not discovered at installation.

Colour & Proof Standards

Deliver artwork in PDF/X-4 or TIFF, ISO Coated v2 CMYK profile, at 1:10 or 1:5 scale with 50 mm bleed on all sides. Physical colour proof is mandatory for any order above ₹40,000 production value — view proof in both ambient daylight and artificial light before sign-off. Maximum acceptable Delta E from brand PMS reference: ΔE ≤ 3. Fonts must be outlined or embedded in the supply file.

Lifespan & Maintenance

Correctly specified UV-coated flex on a proper MS frame: 18–24 months. The primary failure mode is colour fade (UV exposure) and substrate scalloping (inadequate tension). Post-monsoon inspection in October is mandatory — panels that show visible scalloping, colour shift, or legibility loss must be replaced before the Diwali season, which is the highest real estate search window in the Indian calendar.

The Under-Specification Problem

The single most common production failure in Indian real estate outdoor: specifying 280 gsm flex (banner grade) because it saves ₹8–12 per sq ft. The saving is recovered within 6 months when the banner scallops and needs replacement. On a 100-panel perimeter, that saving costs ₹3–4 lakh in premature reprints and an unknown cost in brand perception erosion.

Specify substrate grade, ink type, lamination micron, and frame pipe dimensions in the purchase order — not verbally. Vendors will supply to the minimum viable specification if the order does not mandate the standard.

File Delivery Checklist — Vendor Brief

PDF/X-4 or TIFF · ISO Coated v2 · Scale 1:10 or 1:5
50 mm bleed all sides · crop marks included
All fonts outlined or embedded · no live text
Physical proof mandatory above ₹40,000 order value
Seam position marked on layout before approval
Installation photograph submitted to brand records within 48 hours of completion
Substrate 02 / 03
Backlit /
Translucent
The perception upgrade. Backlit instantly elevates a sales office or gate board from site infrastructure to brand statement.
◈

Substrate & Print Standards

510 gsm translucent PVC substrate. Never eco-solvent on translucent — eco-solvent inks bleed on backlit material and produce a halo effect around type. Use UV or latex ink only. Minimum print resolution at final size: 100–150 dpi — backlit amplifies every resolution artefact that flex printing at 60 dpi conceals. A mandatory backlit proof — viewed both in daylight and after dark — is non-negotiable before authorising the full production run.

The CMYK Backlit Shift Problem

Backlit transmission shifts perceived colour significantly toward cyan. A navy blue approved from a print proof appears blue-green on the lightbox. Brand colours must be proofed specifically on backlit substrate with the actual LED source temperature before any production approval. The standard practice of sending a backlit colour profile is insufficient — only a physical backlit proof with the actual LED reveals the true output.

Lightbox & Frame Selection

SEG (Silicone Edge Graphics) aluminium lightbox is the preferred system for premium applications — tool-free fabric change, consistent edge tension, and no visible frame. Snap-frame systems are acceptable for secondary placements. Avoid thin-profile 35 mm frames on boards above 6×3 ft — the frame bow under fabric tension creates perimeter shadows that degrade the lit appearance.

LED Lighting Specification

LED T8 strips or LED panels, minimum 3000K colour temperature (warm white for parchment-palette brand systems; 4000K for steel-and-white visual systems). Uniformity requirement: ΔU ≤ 15% — visible hotspots or dark zones are a production failure, not an acceptable tolerance. Power budget: 5W per sq ft of illuminated face. Isolator and MCB mandatory at each lightbox; SPD (surge protection device) at the distribution board.

Approved Applications

  • Sales office fascia and primary name board
  • Main construction gate board (premium positioning)
  • Model apartment entrance signage
  • Experience centre interior feature walls
  • Project launch event backdrop panels
Substrate 03 / 03
LED Display /
Digital
The dynamic medium. Content updates in real time. Requires the most rigorous specification, structural engineering, and regulatory compliance.
◉

Module Specification

Outdoor installations: P6 module pitch (6 mm) minimum for viewing distances of 5 m or beyond. P4 or P3 for close-range installations (3 m or less, typically sales office interiors). Brightness: minimum 5,000 nits outdoor with automatic ambient light sensor for dimming — a display running at full brightness at night is a liability, not an asset. Refresh rate: minimum 1,920 Hz to eliminate flicker in photographs and video coverage of the project.

Content & Infrastructure

Cloud-managed CMS with 4G and WiFi dual-link is the minimum infrastructure standard — single-link systems create content update failures that leave error screens visible on a premium brand surface. Content format: 1080p MP4 or HTML5 for dynamic content; JPG static fallback mandatory for CMS connectivity failures. Cabinet: die-cast aluminium, IP65 for outdoor, IP54 for semi-enclosed (covered car park canopy). Viewing angle: H:140° V:140° minimum.

Structural & Electrical Requirements

Stabilised 3-phase power supply for installations above 8 sq m. UPS backup mandatory for sales office LED displays — a CMS error on the sales office screen during a buyer site visit is an avoidable brand failure. Engineering certificate mandatory for any outdoor LED installation above 20 sq m total face area. Certificate must be available on site during any municipal or CEIG inspection.

Regulatory Requirements

MCGM (Mumbai) requires a separate digital sign licence for LED displays, distinct from the standard advertisement licence. BBMP (Bengaluru) also requires a specific digital hoarding licence. Factor 4–8 weeks for licence processing into the project schedule. LED displays above 10 sq m require an electrical safety certificate signed off by a CEIG-approved electrician — a separate requirement from the structural engineering certificate.

Content Standards

  • No flashing, strobing, or animation faster than 2 Hz
  • Night brightness must reduce automatically — 5,000 nit + after 8 PM is a complaint trigger
  • Static fallback image must be brand-compliant — not a blue error screen
  • Content loop duration: 15–30 seconds per creative for outdoor
  • All RERA-required disclosures must appear in static content slots

Vertical & Elevated Identity

Crane Banners & Scaffolding Wraps — The Sky Is Not Empty

The most under-leveraged brand surfaces in real estate construction. A crane at 40 metres is a 360-degree brand flag visible from 1.5 kilometres. A scaffolding wrap converts a construction eyesore into a kilometre-scale hoarding at near-zero incremental structure cost. Both formats are entirely absent from most developers' brand planning.

Elevated Format 01
Crane
Banner
The highest brand touchpoint on the site. Visible from 1.5 km. No copy — logo mark only. Engineering approval mandatory.
↑

The Structural Constraint

Crane banners require signed coordination with the crane operator and a structural loading assessment from a certified engineer. The critical rule: no banner may exceed 3% of the crane mast's wind resistance specification without a formal loading certificate. This is not a bureaucratic requirement — it is a safety calculation. An over-loaded crane banner in a coastal city at 50+ km/h wind can cause a structural incident. The brand is not worth that risk.

The Content Rule at Elevation

Crane branding is brand mark only. The logo at 6 sq m visible from 1 km needs no supporting text. Adding project name as a secondary band is acceptable in a single weight below the logo. Adding marketing copy, floor counts, RERA numbers, or pricing at crane elevation is a category error — none of that content is legible at the viewing distance, and attempting it makes the board look like a failed compromise between two communication objectives.

Production Specification

  • Substrate: 270 gsm mesh PVC, 40% open area minimum
  • Maximum panel width: 3 m (single piece without seam)
  • Fixing: Ratchet strap through eyelets + secondary safety cable
  • Anti-furl: Stainless steel rod in bottom hem pocket
  • Wind load design: IS 875 Part 3, Vb = 50 m/s
  • Logo print height: minimum 1.5 m on a 3 m panel
  • Ink: Solvent or UV, maximum 3 colours (brand primary on white)

Night Visibility Option

Retro-reflective ink or retroreflective vinyl applied to the logo mark extends crane banner visibility through night hours and during morning/evening site illumination. This is particularly effective for sites adjacent to highways or elevated roads where the crane is visible beyond daylight hours. Retro-reflective treatment adds approximately 35–40% to production cost but multiplies the effective impression hours of the placement.

Coordination Checklist

  • Written approval from crane operator and site civil engineer
  • Wind load calculation for specific crane model and banner dimensions
  • Banner weight calculation included in crane lift capacity check
  • Removal protocol agreed before installation — who removes it, when
  • Insurance coverage confirmed for banner as site equipment
  • Inspect fixing points after first high-wind event post-installation
Elevated Format 02
Scaffolding
Wrap
Construction eyesore converted to brand canvas. One kilometre of visual presence at the cost of replacing safety netting with branded mesh.
◫

The Module Design Logic

Scaffolding wraps must be designed to work with the scaffold tube grid — not against it. The scaffold tube pitch imposes a fixed module. Design in 2 m × 3 m panels that align to the tube spacing; avoid full-face seamless designs that become a visual failure at every tube join. The tube grid is not a constraint to be hidden — it is the design module to be accepted. The best scaffold wraps use the module as a design element.

Content Rule at Scaffold Scale

Scaffolding wraps are a Tier II surface. They carry: logo mark, project name, tagline, and a single visual. Pricing, floor counts, RERA numbers, and contact details must never appear on scaffolding wraps. These are Tier III gate board elements — deploying them at scaffold elevation produces illegible compliance text viewed from 30+ metres. The wrap also carries the DGFASLI mandatory fire-retardant fabric requirement — this is a safety regulation, not optional.

Three Wrap Design Modes

  • Mode A — Brand Colour Field: 90% brand primary colour with logo mark. Best for dense urban sites with competing visual noise. High recognition, highly ownable.
  • Mode B — Rendered Lifestyle: Architectural render + brand band. Best for luxury projects with arterial frontage. Risk: render quality must be exceptional — poor renders at scale destroy premium positioning.
  • Mode C — Pattern + Badge: Geometric brand pattern field + logo badge. Best for long scaffold runs and mid-construction phases. Scalable and seam-forgiving.

Production Specification

  • Substrate: 340 gsm mesh PVC, 35% open area minimum
  • Design module: 2 m × 3 m repeating panel tile
  • Fixing: Cable ties through scaffold pipe + velcro secondary
  • Edge: Sewn 50 mm hem, brass eyelets every 30 cm
  • Coverage: 60–70% of scaffold face — not 100%
  • Fire rating: DGFASLI FR fabric certificate mandatory
  • Print resolution: 60 dpi at 1:1 final scale

Design From One Direction

Design scaffolding wraps to read from one primary approach direction. Do not attempt a coherent image across the full building face — scaffold geometry will defeat it at every seam. Design for pattern and brand colour fields instead of pictorial content. The one exception: Mode B rendered lifestyle, where the render can be scaled across multiple panels if the seam positions are controlled at the design stage.

The Seam Failure Mode

The most common scaffold wrap failure: designing a full-face photographic image without mapping seam positions against the scaffold tube grid. The render of a beautiful tower face becomes a patchwork of misaligned panels with each tube visible as a visible disruption. Commissioning a scaffold wrap without a site survey to establish the exact tube pitch is a production error, not a creative choice.

Post-Monsoon Maintenance Protocol

October inspection: check all cable tie fixings — replace any that show UV brittleness
Re-tension all velcro secondary fixings — monsoon wind cycling loosens them over 3 months
Inspect all hem eyelets for pull-through — reinforce any that show deformation
Photograph full wrap from primary viewing distance — compare against original installation record
Replace panels where colour shift exceeds visible comparison to reference panels stored on site

First and Last Impression

Construction Gate Branding — Regulated, Permanent, and Conversion-Ready

The construction gate is the brand's most regulated sign, its most inspected surface, and the first physical touchpoint for every buyer, contractor, and lender who arrives at the site. It must function as a compliance board, a brand statement, and a sales tool simultaneously — and these three requirements must be zoned, not merged.

Gate Format 01
Main Gate
Identity Board
RERA-mandated, permanently installed, updated within 30 days of any regulatory amendment. Not flex on a temporary frame.
⊞

The Four-Zone Architecture

Every construction gate board must be structured in four discrete zones — Crown (top 20%): developer logo + project name + tagline, brand primary background; Identity (middle 40%): project visual or render + key USP statement, hero image or brand accent; Compliance (lower 25%): RERA number, authority approval references, approvals list, neutral white or light panel background; Conversion (bottom strip 15%): phone number, website, QR code, sales office address, high-contrast dark band. These zones must never be merged or reordered.

Material Permanence Standard

The main gate board is required by RERA to be maintained in legible condition throughout the construction period. This requirement disqualifies flex vinyl on a temporary frame as the primary gate board material — flex degrades in 18 months and the RERA obligation runs for the full construction duration. ACP (4 mm aluminium composite panel) with UV digital print and lamination, or 18-gauge MS sheet with baked enamel, is the minimum durability standard. The board must be legible from 10 metres at night.

RERA Compliance Zone Requirements

The compliance zone must include: RERA registration number at minimum 50 mm print height; project completion date as registered; approving authority name and licence/approval number; statement that the project is registered under state RERA with the portal URL. Any amendment to these details requires RERA amendment filing first, then board update within the mandated window — typically 30 days. Never update the board before the RERA portal reflects the change.

Physical Installation Standard

  • Board size: 8×4 ft (single gate) / 12×6 ft (double vehicular gate)
  • Substrate: 4 mm ACP with UV print + lamination, or 18g MS with baked enamel
  • Mounting: Welded MS angle frame with RCC footing — no temporary post-and-clip
  • Illumination: Directional spot or backlit lightbox preferred
  • QR code: Minimum 8×8 cm print size, UTM-tracked to campaign analytics
  • Phone number: Minimum 80 mm print height in conversion zone

Gate Wing Panels & Entry Gantry

Gate wing panels are purely brand territory — no compliance content required or permitted. Use this surface for lifestyle photography, architectural renders, or amenity visuals. For large sites (5+ acres), a branded entry gantry over the vehicular access road is the premium format: minimum 5.5 m clearance height, MS hollow section structure (galvanised), ACP panel cladding or tensioned fabric, integrated LED profile. Content: project name and brand mark only.

Complete Signage Taxonomy

The Siteyard Signage Family — All Nine Formats, Every Dimension

A fully branded siteyard has no anonymous signage. Every functional sign — from the tower identification board to the borewell caution label — follows the same typographic and colour system, scaled to its tier. This section specifies each member of the siteyard signage family with dimensions, substrate, placement logic, and content rules.

Universal Rule — Safety Signs Are Exempt From Brand Redesign

ISO 7010 safety signs (Signs 06 and below) must never be redesigned with project brand colours or typography. The international colour coding — red for prohibition, yellow for warning, green for safe condition, blue for mandatory action — is a life-safety standard. A branded hard-hat sign that replaces safety red with brand navy is not a branding achievement. It is a liability. Brand consistency at Tiers I–III; ISO 7010 compliance at Tier IV — these are not competing objectives.

Sign 01 · Tier III

Main Project Identity Board

10×5 ft standard · 12×6 ft large sites

Developer brand mark, project name, tagline, RERA compliance zone, and CTA zone. ACP with UV print. Permanent installation at main entry. Updated on RERA amendments within mandated window.

Sign 02 · Tier III

Tower / Block Identification

4×2 ft · One per tower base per floor

Block or tower name, building designation, floor count and number. Fixed to building base or column. Replaced with permanent building signage on possession commencement.

Sign 03 · Tier III

Directional / Wayfinding

3×1.5 ft · Arrow-mounted on post

Single destination per sign. Arrow + destination name + distance. Brand colour fascia. Consistent typeface only — no custom script. Placed at every decision point from site entry.

Sign 04 · Tier III

Zone Identification Board

4×2 ft · Per zone boundary

Zone name + zone code + access restriction. Colour-coded left stripe by zone type: green for safe access, amber for restricted, red for danger. ISO 7010 colour coding — not brand colours.

Sign 05 · Tier III

Officer / Contact Board

3×2 ft · Sales office + PM office

Name, designation, contact number. Updated at personnel changes via laminated insert panel — ACP frame with replaceable insert is preferred. Required by RERA at all sales contact points.

Sign 06 · Tier IV

Mandatory Safety Signs

60×45 cm · ISO 7010 standard

ISO 7010 compliant safety signs on reflective vinyl substrate. Bilingual — English + Tamil / Hindi / Kannada. Never customised with brand colours. Hard hat, high-vis, safety footwear, and restricted entry signs mandatory at all entry points.

Sign 07 · Tier IV

RERA Disclosure Panel

24×18 inches · Compliance format only

RERA registration number, project completion date, approving authority name. Mandatory size and format per state RERA regulation. Cannot be styled to brand aesthetics — regulated content and layout govern. Separate from main gate board.

Sign 08 · Tier IV

Statutory Approvals Board

4×3 ft · Fixed at gate · Permanent

Environmental clearance, fire NOC, structural approval reference numbers. Required for sites above 5,000 sq m built-up area. Separate board from RERA panel. Updated within 30 days of any approval amendment.

Sign 09 · Tier III

Construction Progress Milestone

4×3 ft · Updated quarterly

Current floor count, milestone phase, and handover status. Updated per seasonal protocol. Visible to buyers and neighbours. The most powerful trust-building sign on the site — evidence that the developer tracks and communicates progress.

Content Freeze Rules — What Can Be Updated, and What Cannot

Content governance on site boards is not solely a brand question. Several content elements are legal commitments under RERA — changing them without the corresponding regulatory filing creates a compliance violation that can result in penalties, project registration suspension, and adverse publicity that no marketing spend can offset.

Update Freely

Marketing & Campaign Content

Campaign messaging, seasonal offer inserts, lifestyle photography, tagline variants, and visual treatments may be updated at any time by the brand team. QR code UTM parameters: update annually to maintain analytics accuracy. Contact numbers: update immediately on any change — a disconnected site number is a conversion failure.

Never Update Without Filing First

RERA-Governed Content

RERA registration number (immutable), total unit count, land area as disclosed, project completion date, and promoter name as registered — all require RERA amendment filing before any board update. The correct sequence is: file the amendment → receive confirmation → update the board within the mandated window. Never reverse this sequence.

The Central RERA Mandate

RERA requires the project registration number to appear on all marketing materials — including outdoor advertising. The exact size, placement, and format requirements are governed by state RERA rules, which vary. Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Telangana each have specific disclosure format requirements. In all cases, the RERA number must be in a legible size, accompanied by the project name as registered and the project completion date.

CREDAI Ethical Advertising Standards

CREDAI member developers are bound by ethical advertising standards that prohibit: inflated FAR or area claims, occupancy projections inconsistent with RERA filings, use of "approved" without documentary evidence, and competitor disparagement in any form. These apply to outdoor advertising. The CREDAI standard recommends prominent RERA number display on all outdoor advertising — which is consistent with statutory requirement in all four major jurisdictions.

MCGM — Greater Mumbai

  • Advertisement Licence mandatory before any hoarding installation
  • Standard licence: up to 40 sq ft without special NOC
  • LED digital display: separate digital sign licence required
  • Prohibited: within 100 m of national monument or heritage structure
  • Wind load certificate mandatory for any structure above 60 sq ft
  • Structural certificate required above 4.5 m total height
  • Maharashtra RERA: Board at main entry, minimum 1 sq m, all disclosures

CMDA / GCC — Chennai

  • GCC Advertisement Permission required — apply to zonal office
  • Road category governs maximum size: major arterial roads max 40 sq ft without engineer certificate
  • Tamil Nadu Outdoor Advertisement Policy 2014 governs format and content
  • Heritage zone restriction: no hoardings within 500 m of protected structures
  • No political messaging association — even implicit
  • TN RERA: Entry board + one board per marketing office; TNRERA number + QR to RERA portal required

BBMP — Bengaluru

  • BBMP Advertisement Licence + structural NOC required
  • BBMP Advertisement Byelaws 2006 govern all outdoor installations
  • Standard limit: 12 sq m single-face without special permission
  • Engineer certificate for any structure above 3 m height (lower threshold than MCGM)
  • LED hoarding: separate digital licence in addition to standard licence
  • Karnataka RERA: Board within 7 days of registration; KRERA number + QR mandatory
HMDA — Hyderabad

Conditional compliance

Site boards within building permission NOC are often covered — verify for each project. Roadside hoardings need separate GHMC licence. No advertising within 300 m of national highway without NHAI NOC.
RERA Compliance Copy Block

Mandatory on all Tier II–III boards

Every site board must include: RERA registration number, project name as registered, completion date, state RERA portal URL, and the statement that specifications are subject to change and this is not an offer to purchase.
Structural Certificate Threshold

All three metros: 4.5 m height

Any hoarding structure above 4.5 m total height requires a structural stability certificate from a registered structural engineer. Certificate must be available on site during any inspection.
LED Electrical Safety

All metros: above 10 sq m

LED displays above 10 sq m require electrical safety certificate from a CEIG-approved electrician. This is separate from and in addition to the structural engineering certificate.

The following checklist covers the full production lifecycle. Phase 1 through Phase 4 are sequential — no phase begins before the previous phase is signed off.

QA and Ongoing Governance

  • Installation supervised by brand team representative

  • LED brightness, uniformity, and colour temperature verified on site
  • Installation completion photographs submitted to developer brand records
  • Monthly visual inspection scheduled and assigned to site manager
  • Post-monsoon full substrate inspection in October — mandatory annual milestone
  • Any RERA amendment reflected on gate board within mandated window
  • Annual licence renewal for municipal advertisement permissions tracked
