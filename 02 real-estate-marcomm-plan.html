01
Where You Stand Before You Plan

Situation Analysis

Every marketing plan begins not with ambition, but with honest assessment. The situation analysis gives the plan its strategic foundation — without it, objectives float without gravity.

The 4-Layer Analysis Framework
Layer 01

Market Analysis

Current state of the local real estate market — price trends, inventory supply, demand drivers, infrastructure pipeline, competitive launches in the micro-market. Use JLL, Anarock, PropEquity data. Identify whether it's a buyer's or seller's market right now.

Layer 02

Competitor Analysis

Map your top 3–5 competitors: their current campaigns, channel spend mix, pricing, inventory status, brand positioning, and customer sentiment (reviews). What are they saying? What are they NOT saying? Where are the gaps you can own?

Layer 03

Brand & Sales Audit

Current lead volume, CPL by channel, site-visit-to-booking conversion, channel partner performance, digital health (website traffic, SEO ranking, review score), and the last 6 months of campaign ROI. What is working? What is burning money?

Layer 04

Buyer Intelligence

Who are your actual buyers vs your assumed buyers? Use CRM data to build real buyer profiles: age, income, geography, family structure, purchase motivation (end-use vs investment), source of awareness, key objections, decision timeline.

Layer 05

SWOT Synthesis

Strengths (what the brand/project genuinely owns), Weaknesses (honest gaps in delivery, reputation, location, pricing), Opportunities (market gaps, competitor vulnerabilities, infrastructure tailwinds), Threats (macro-economic, regulatory, competitive).

Layer 06

Regulatory Landscape

RERA status of the project, any pending approvals, GST implications on pricing communication, ASCI advertising guidelines for real estate (no misleading claims, mandatory disclosures), and local municipal communication rules for hoardings and banners.

Decision Stakeholder Map
Primary

The Decision-Maker

The person who signs the cheque and drives the search. Typically male, 32–50. Motivated by financial logic and status. Needs ROI data, builder credibility proof, and location rationale.

High Influence

The Spouse / Partner

Often the emotional decision-maker. Responds to kitchen design, natural light, neighbourhood safety, school proximity, and community feel. The model flat experience must primarily win this person.

Advisory

Parents

Still highly influential in Indian real estate, especially for first-time buyers. Concerned with builder longevity, documentation correctness, vastu, location safety, and resale value. Needs trust signals, not aspirational content.

Channel

Channel Partner / Broker

A critical audience in their own right. They need brand training, exclusive previews, competitive commission structures, fast response from builder team, and marketing material that makes their job easier.

Amplifier

Current Residents

Your most credible brand ambassadors. Existing buyers who are happy refer 2–5 buyers each on average. Must be cultivated through community events, anniversary touches, and a structured referral programme with real incentives.

Influencer

Employer / Peer Network

Colleagues, managers, and WhatsApp groups are major real estate information channels. "Where does X live?" is a significant purchase signal. Location prestige and community quality drive peer influence.

Channel Partner Communication Plan
Onboarding

CP Brand Induction

Every channel partner must be a brand ambassador before they are a salesperson. Onboarding kit: brand story, project facts, objection handling guide, do's/don'ts for representing the brand. 2-hour brand induction session mandatory.

Enablement

Sales Toolkit

Digital and physical sales kit: digital brochure, co-branded proposal template, virtual tour link, pricing matrix, EMI calculator, WhatsApp broadcast content ready to deploy, social media post templates with brand compliance.

Engagement

Weekly WhatsApp update on inventory status. Monthly CP meet. Quarterly CP appreciation event. Leaderboard with commission tracking. Priority access to new inventory. Escalation line direct to senior sales team.

When Things Go Wrong — and They Will

Crisis Communication

In real estate, crises are not exceptional — they are structural. Construction delays, legal disputes, media exposure, social media complaints. Every plan needs a response architecture before it is ever needed.

Crisis Scenario Map & Response Framework
● High Severity

Construction Delay

Most common real estate crisis. Response: proactive communication (not reactive). Send a personal letter from MD/CEO within 24 hours of identifying a delay. State the new date and the reason honestly. Offer compensation where applicable (as per RERA). Do NOT communicate through sales team — it must come from leadership. Never go silent.

● High Severity

Legal / RERA Complaint Campaign

If an organised buyer complaint goes public or to media. Designate a single spokesperson (never multiple). Brief legal counsel and communications team simultaneously. Respond to media with facts and a solution path — not denial. Proactively reach out to other buyers with a reassurance letter. Social media monitoring must increase to real-time.

● High Severity

Viral Negative Social Media

A buyer-posted video complaint that begins trending. Do NOT delete or dismiss. First response must be within 2 hours acknowledging the concern. Take conversation to DM and resolve. Post a follow-up public response within 24 hours showing resolution. Brief the sales team immediately — they will face calls. Review the root cause and fix it operationally.

● Medium Severity

Negative Media Coverage

A journalist writes a critical piece — poor quality claims, financial allegations, area negativity. Immediately contact the journalist with verified facts. If inaccurate, request a correction or right of reply. Prepare a counter-narrative with third-party validation (RERA certificate, IGBC rating, buyer testimonials). Do not engage aggressively in public — it amplifies.

● Medium Severity

Price Revision Backlash

Existing buyers react negatively to price increases for new inventory. Communicate price revision to existing buyers first — before public announcement. Frame it as a market appreciation signal that validates their own investment. Offer early-bird pricing for referrals as a loyalty benefit. Never make existing buyers feel they overpaid.

● Manageable

Competitor Negative Campaign

A competitor runs comparative advertising that positions your brand poorly. Do not engage in public mud-slinging. Respond with a brand campaign that amplifies your genuine strengths. If the comparison is factually false, pursue ASCI complaint. Brief your sales and CP team with a factual response kit for when buyers raise it.

The Golden Rules of Real Estate Crisis Communication
#RuleWhy It Matters
01Respond within 2 hours, resolve within 24 hoursEvery hour of silence on social media is interpreted as guilt. Speed is empathy.
02One spokesperson. Always.Multiple voices in a crisis create contradiction and amplify the story.
03Acknowledge before you explainBuyers in distress need to feel heard before they will listen to your explanation.
04Never lie to a journalistA lie discovered post-crisis destroys more than the original crisis. Give facts or say "no comment."
05Over-communicate to existing buyers during a crisisYour buyers are your most important audience. If they hear about the crisis from media first, trust collapses.
06Fix the root cause, then the communicationA crisis that is communicated but not resolved will produce a second, worse crisis.
07Brief your sales team and CPs before going publicThey will face buyer questions. An unprepared sales team in a crisis makes things worse.
