Logo System

Construction Grid & Clear Space Rules

The logo is the most reproduced brand asset. Every dimension, every relationship, every minimum size is defined here to prevent the accumulated damage of inconsistent reproduction across 30+ touchpoints.

Logo Construction Grid — Proportional System

The logo is built on a modular grid where the base unit X equals the height of the wordmark cap-height. All spacing, proportions, and element relationships derive from this unit. Never distort, never rearrange.

Grid Unit X

The Base Measure

All proportional relationships in the logo are expressed as multiples of unit X, which equals the cap-height of the primary wordmark. This makes the system resolution-independent and scaleable from a 16px favicon to a 40m hoarding.

Mark : Wordmark Ratio

1 : 3.5 Optical Ratio

The logo mark occupies a zone 1X wide. The wordmark follows at 3.5X. The horizontal gap between them is fixed at 0.5X. Do not alter this ratio — the mark and wordmark were designed as a unit at this specific proportion.

Tagline Relationship

0.4X Below Wordmark

When the tagline is present, it sits 0.4X below the wordmark baseline, set at 35% of the wordmark point size in the same font, tracked at +350 units. The tagline is never larger than 40% of the wordmark size.

Logo Configurations — Four Approved Lockups
Config A
Horizontal Primary
Default lockup for all standard usage — digital headers, letterheads, email signatures, print ads.
Config B
Stacked
Square-format usage — social avatars, stamp applications, merchandise, square hoardings.
Config C
Mark Only
Approved only when brand is already established in context — favicons, watermarks, embossing, registered trademark situations.
Config D
Reversed White
Dark background applications — site hoardings, presentation covers, event backdrops, dark-mode digital.
Clear Space Rules — The Exclusion Zone
Clear Space Rule

Minimum 0.5X on All Sides

The exclusion zone surrounding the logo equals 0.5X (half the cap-height unit) on every side. No other element — typography, photography, graphic, rule, or decorative element — may enter this zone. On hoardings where space is genuinely constrained, the minimum acceptable clear space is 0.33X but this requires written approval.

Minimum Reproduction Sizes

Digital: 120px · Print: 25mm · Emboss: 15mm

Below these sizes, the logo degrades — fine strokes drop out in print, letter-spacing collapses, and the mark loses its architectural structure. At minimum sizes, use Config C (mark only) or increase the application size. Never attempt to force the horizontal lockup into a small-format application.

Colour Usage Ratios — The 60 · 30 · 10 Rule

60% — Foundation

Cream (#FAF7F2) and Parchment (#F5F0E8) dominate every layout. These neutrals are the canvas — they ensure the brand feels light, considered, and premium. Most of any page's white space is one of these two tones.

30% — Structure

Brand Black (#0D0D0B) and Sand (#E8DFC8) provide structural contrast. Black anchors headings, body text, and the dark hero sections. Sand appears in backgrounds, alternating rows, and hover states.

10% — Accent

Gold, Rust, Sage, and Steel are deployed sparingly as accent colours. Their rarity is what gives them emphasis power. When everything is gold, nothing is. Gold dominates the 10% — Rust, Sage, and Steel divide the remainder.

Photography Direction

Visual Language & Shot Archetypes

Photography is the brand's most emotionally immediate communication tool. A misaligned image — over-lit, over-styled, or generically sourced — signals inauthenticity faster than any other brand failure. These are the specific visual standards for every photography application.

The Six Photography Archetypes — Real Estate Applications
Archetype 01
Architectural Exterior

Architecture Exterior

The building as sculpture. Shot at golden hour (6–8am or 5–7pm) with natural warm light raking across facade. Camera angle: 3/4 view at eye level or slightly below — never looking down on the building. Sky must have character — blue hour preferred for premium applications.

Golden hour 3/4 angle Eye level or below Drone nadir Midday flat light
Archetype 02
Interior Living Spaces

Interior Living

Inhabited but not cluttered. One dominant light source (window), warm supplement from side. Focal point is always a lifestyle element — a book, a coffee cup, a single plant — never a TV remote or phone charger. Colour palette: neutrals with one warm accent. No synthetic 3D render substitutes in premium publications.

Natural light dominant Lifestyle props Warm colour temp Cluttered styling CGI renders for print
Archetype 03
Amenity Spaces

Amenity & Lifestyle

The promise of a life well-lived. Shot to convey experience, not square footage. Pool: blue hour, reflections in water, no people or one silhouette. Gym: early morning light, clean equipment, no clutter. Clubhouse: event-set or post-event with beautiful residue — glasses, flowers, candlelight.

Blue hour for pools Mood over information Midday pool photos Equipment branding visible
Archetype 04
Resident Portraits

Resident & People

Real residents, not models. Shot in their actual home or the project's amenity spaces. Natural expressions — doing something, not posing. Window light from the side. Depth of field: subject sharp, background softly out of focus. Diversity of age and family structure — not just young couples. Testimonials must use original photography, never stock.

Real residents Natural light Activity, not posing Stock library people Over-directed smiling
Archetype 05
Location & Context

Location & Context

The neighbourhood as a stage. Aerial or elevated shots at twilight that show the project in its full urban context. Reveals infrastructure connections (metro, highway, IT parks) without being purely functional. The image should make the buyer feel the location's energy, not just understand its map coordinates.

Twilight / blue hour Elevated perspective Context visible Flat Google Maps perspective Daytime drone nadir
Archetype 06
Material Details

Material & Detail

The evidence of quality. Macro photography of materials and finishes — imported stone, brass hardware, timber grain, woven fabric, handmade tile. Shallow depth of field with one plane in sharp focus. Lighting: single directional source at 20–30° angle to reveal texture. These images are used in brochure gutter pages, digital carousels, and site office displays to justify price premium.

Macro, one sharp plane Directional texture light Premium materials only Wide-angle of materials Flash-lit flat materials
Photography Technical Specifications
✓ Always Do
Shoot in RAW format at minimum 36MP for print applications
Brief the photographer with this manual and a mood board before shoot day
Shoot at golden hour (6–8am / 5–7pm) for all exterior architecture
Use a colour calibration card at the start of every shoot for consistent grading
Obtain model release forms for all people featured, including background subjects
Deliver final images in sRGB for digital and Adobe RGB for print
Maintain an image library with keywords, orientation, and approved usage rights documented
Retouch only to remove temporary distractions (scaffolding, bins, construction hoardings)
✗ Never Do
Use stock photography for exterior, interior, or resident imagery in premium collateral
Apply HDR or over-processed tone mapping that creates artificial vibrancy
Composite blue skies into daytime photos — shoot at the right time instead
Retouch away permanent site features (overhead power lines, adjacent buildings that will remain)
Use 3D renders in place of photography in printed brochures for delivered/possession-stage projects
Apply Instagram-style filters or heavily desaturated "moody" grading — the brand palette is warm, not cool
Crop people at the ankles, wrists, or mid-face — always complete the human form
Use photography commissioned for one project in another project's marketing without retaking

Iconography System

Icon Style, Grid & Real Estate Library

Icons are the smallest unit of the brand identity system. When inconsistent — some filled, some outlined, some rounded, some sharp — they shatter the visual coherence that brochures, apps, and digital ads work to build. One style. One grid. One library.

Icon Style Specifications — The Single Approved Style

Line / Stroke Style

All icons are drawn in 1.5pt stroke weight at 36×36px base grid. No fills. No mixed strokes. Stroke cap: round. Stroke join: round for organic icons, miter for architectural icons. Corners: 1.5px radius minimum on all rectangular shapes.

Styles to Avoid

Filled solid icons, two-tone coloured icons, rounded "bubbly" icon styles (Apple-inspired), ultra-thin hairline icons, 3D isometric icons, and inconsistently mixed styles within any single application are all unapproved. If an icon exists in a competitor's kit in a similar style, it should not be used.

Icon Grid — 36×36px

All icons are designed on a 36×36px grid with a 2px safe zone on all sides, giving a 32×32px drawing area. For small applications (16px, 24px), icons must be redrawn at the target size — never simply scaled down, as stroke weights will become incorrect. Pixel-perfect alignment on whole-number coordinates only.

Touchpoint Specifications

Dimensions, Safe Zones & Production Standards

The brand manifests across 32 distinct touchpoints. For each one: the exact dimensions, the production format, the logo configuration, the minimum safety margins, and the single rule that must be followed. When in doubt, reference this section before briefing any supplier.

TP-01

Website — Desktop Header

Fixed navigation bar, 64px height minimum. Logo in Config A (horizontal) at 160px wide minimum. Nav links in Syne Regular 13pt. Background: cream or transparent-over-hero. Sticky on scroll.

Email Template

600px fixed width. Header band in Brand Black, logo Config D reversed. Body on white #FFFFFF, not cream — for inbox rendering consistency. Single column for mobile. Footer: unsubscribe + RERA in DM Mono 9pt.

Meta Ads — Social Formats

Four approved ratios. All must carry RERA number in DM Mono 8pt at minimum. Brand must occupy top-left or bottom-left zone. No more than 20% of image area as text overlay (Meta policy + brand standard).

Seven approved sizes. Dark background configurations strongly preferred for brand consistency and click-through performance. Logo must be visible on all sizes including the smallest (200×200px). All assets must be delivered in both static and animated (15s max) versions.

Primary large-format brand touchpoint. Dark background mandatory. Logo Config D (reversed) minimum 300mm wide. Headline in Cormorant Garamond Light minimum 96pt equivalent at viewing distance.
